# American Moto Co — Brand Voice Guide

## Core Identity

**What AMC is:** A motorcycle culture media brand that tells stories from the road — no matter what you ride.

**What AMC is NOT:** A gear review site, a brand fanboy outlet, a gatekeeping community, or a corporate motorcycle publication.

## Voice Characteristics

| Trait | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Conversational** | Reads like a friend telling you a story over coffee, not a press release | "She wasn't looking for an adventure. The adventure found her." |
| **Warm** | Inclusive, welcoming, no jargon or insider-only language | "Whether you ride a Grom or a Goldwing, you belong here." |
| **Editorial** | Structured and intentional, not rambling — every paragraph earns its place | Clean transitions, pull quotes, narrative arc |
| **Subject-forward** | Let riders speak in their own words — long direct quotes, minimal editorializing | Block quotes from interviews are the centerpiece |
| **Lightly humorous** | Occasional wit, never try-hard or sarcastic | Italic asides, dry observations |
| **Anti-gatekeeping** | The defining value. No brand tribalism, no experience requirements | "No matter what you ride" appears everywhere |

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Use the rider's own words as much as possible
- Write headlines that hint at the story, not clickbait
- Include specific details (bike model, road name, mile marker)
- Let the photography do heavy lifting — write around it, not over it
- Use "riders" not "bikers" (more inclusive, less tribal)
- Reference the community as "we" and "us"

### Don't
- Gatekeep by brand ("real riders ride X")
- Use motorcycle jargon without context (not everyone knows what a "low-side" is)
- Editorialize on someone else's story — let them tell it
- Use aggressive or exclusionary language
- Pad articles — if the story is 4 minutes, it's 4 minutes
- Use stock phrases ("the open road calls" — be more specific)

## Taglines

- **Primary:** "Breaking down the walls to share the stories from the road, no matter what you ride."
- **Footer:** "Built for riders, who just want to ride."
- **Subscribe CTA:** "Join the Ride" or "Never miss a story from the road"
- **About:** "We're here for the stories, not the brands."

## Content Category Tone

| Category | Tone | Length |
|----------|------|--------|
| Rider Profiles | Intimate, narrative, documentary | Long-form (6+ min) |
| Reviews | Honest, first-person, practical | Mid-length (4-5 min) |
| Epic Rides | Vivid, descriptive, aspirational | Mid-length (4-5 min) |
| Weekly Newsletter | Casual, curated, quick-hitting | Short (2-3 min) |
| Feature Stories | Varies — event coverage, cultural pieces | Varies |

## Photography Direction

- **Real riders, real roads** — no studio shots, no stock
- **Natural light** — golden hour, overcast, documentary feel
- **Slightly desaturated** — warm tones, not oversaturated Instagram look
- **Bikes in context** — on roads, in garages, at rest stops. Not showroom
- **People > machines** — riders' faces, hands on handlebars, boots on pegs
- **Landscape context** — show where the ride happens, not just the bike
